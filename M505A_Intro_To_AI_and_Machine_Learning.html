#using IQR (Inter Quartile Range) method and remove the outliers completely, which will give us more accurate results
def outliers(test_frame, column):
    O1 = test_frame[column].quantile(0.25)
    O3 = test_frame[column].quantile(0.75)
    OR = O3 - O1
    l_limit = O1 - 1.5 * OR
    u_limit = O3 + 1.5 * OR
    new_frame = test_frame[(test_frame[column] >= l_limit) & (test_frame[column] <= u_limit)]
    return new_frame

n_columns = ['release_year']
for column in n_columns:
    amazon = outliers(amazon, column)

Stength And Limitations:¶

The best attribute of the Decision Tree model is that it's easy to understand and interpret. This means the features--if they are categorical or numerical--consistency does not need to be checked. What the algorithm does is the following: it looks for the attribute that has the most relevance. One issue is that if the trees are deep without being cut, then they will tend to over fit the data. Furthermore, Decision Trees are labile in that any slight modification could give a totally different tree. The main preference of these kinds of features is those with multiple levels since they are incapable of generalization. Thus, the formation of prejudiced categories is stimulated. The tough nut to crack at the beginning of the process is that only simple trees, which we can understand, will be created, because complex trees are not accurate enoughtointerpret.
